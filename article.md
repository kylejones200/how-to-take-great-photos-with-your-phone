### How to Take Great Photos With Your Phone You don't need an expensive DSLR to capture sharp, professional-looking photos. Modern iPhones come with excellent cameras and built-in features

that make it possible for anyone to take great shots. The trick is knowing how to use them. Here are four simple techniques that will immediately improve your iPhone photography.
### 1. Compose the Shot Composition sets the foundation for every photo. Use the "rule of thirds." Imagine dividing your frame into three equal parts vertically and horizontally. Place your subject at one of

the intersections for a balanced, natural look. To make this easier, turn on grid lines in **Settings \> Photos & Camera**. Add depth to your photos by keeping the subject close to the camera and including something in the background farther away. If you have an iPhone with **Portrait Mode**, use it to blur the background and make your subject pop. This effect is called *bokeh*, and it's one of the easiest ways to make photos look professional.
### 2. Control the Lighting Lighting matters more than any other factor. Tap and hold your subject's face on

the screen to lock auto-focus and auto-exposure. You'll see **AE/AF LOCK** at the top of your display. If the subject looks too dark, slide your finger upward to brighten the exposure or downward to darken it. And skip the flash whenever you can. Natural light creates far better results.
### 3. Use the Volume Button to Shoot Many photos come out blurry because pressing

the on-screen shutter button moves the phone. Instead, press the **volume button** on the side of your iPhone. It works like the shutter on a traditional camera and keeps your shot steady. For moving subjects --- or to avoid someone blinking --- take multiple shots. Hold down the volume button and your phone will capture a burst of photos. Later, you can select the best one.
### 4. Turn On Geolocation This last tip won't change how your photos look, but it will make them easier to organize. Turn on geolocation in **Settings \> Privacy \> Camera**, then choose "While Using

the App." This saves the location of each photo, so you'll always know where it was taken. These four simple steps --- composition, lighting, steady shooting, and geotagging --- will elevate your iPhone photography instantly. Try them the next time you're at a family gathering, a work event, or out exploring. You'll be surprised at how much sharper and more professional your photos look.
